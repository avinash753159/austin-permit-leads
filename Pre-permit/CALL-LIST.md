## Call Script Framework

Use this for any call. Adapt the specifics.

### Opening (10 seconds)
> "Hey [name/team], I'm Avinash from Brimstone Partner. I track construction projects in Austin at the pre-construction stage — before they hit bid boards or even get permitted."

### The Hook (15 seconds)
> "Right now I'm looking at [specific project] — a [$value] [type] at [location]. It's in the [stage] stage. Since you [do their trade], that's going to need [their specific work]."

### The Ask (10 seconds)
> "Are you tracking that one? I've got [X] projects like that in the pipeline. Want me to send you the list?"

### If They Say Yes
> "Great. What's the best email? I'll send you a one-page report with the top projects — developer names, estimated values, and what stage they're in. I also have a free dashboard where you can see all of this in real time."

### If They Say "We're Good" / Not Interested
> "No worries. If you ever want early intel on what's coming down the pipeline, I'm at (832) 380-5845. Take care."

### If They Ask "What Do You Charge?"
> "The dashboard is free right now — I'm building it out and looking for feedback from people in the industry. If it's useful to you, we can talk about a paid version later. For now I just want to make sure the data is actually helpful."

### If You Get Voicemail
> "Hey [name], this is Avinash Nayak, (832) 380-5845. I track pre-construction projects in Austin — the stuff that's 6 months from permit. Right now there's [$X] in [type] in the pipeline. Give me a call back if you want the project list. Again, (832) 380-5845."

## Rules

1. **Call between 7-9 AM or 2-4 PM.** Construction people start early and are on sites midday.
2. **Stand up when you call.** You'll sound more energetic.
3. **Don't read the script word-for-word.** Know the bullet points, be conversational.
4. **Drop a specific number.** "$7 billion light rail" or "$500 million high-rise" — specifics are what make you credible.
5. **Ask for the email, not the sale.** You're offering to send them something useful, not close a deal.
6. **If they engage, ask:** "What kind of projects are you looking for?" — then you know what to send them.
7. **Follow up the same day.** If they give you an email, send the report within the hour while you're fresh in their mind.
8. **Track every call.** Note who picked up, what they said, and follow-up date.
